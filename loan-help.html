Loan Help

Borrowing from friends is often the best option. But when it's not, we'll help you find the right alternative.

Why Borrow from Friends First?

Peer lending through Vony offers real advantages over traditional loans. Here's why it's worth trying first.

Peer Lending on Vony

  • No credit check — borrowing from a friend doesn't show up on your credit report or affect your score
  • Lower interest — friends typically charge little to no interest compared to 10-36% APR from banks
  • Flexible terms — negotiate repayment schedules that work for both of you
  • No late penalties — restructure payments if life gets in the way, without fees
  • Fast approval — no applications, no waiting periods, no paperwork beyond Vony's simple agreement
  • Builds trust — successful repayment strengthens your relationships and Vony trust profile

Traditional Loans

  • Hard credit inquiry — applications temporarily lower your credit score
  • Higher interest rates — personal loans average 10-36% APR depending on credit
  • Rigid repayment — fixed monthly payments with little room to adjust
  • Late fees and penalties — missed payments incur fees and damage your credit
  • Slower process — applications, verification, and approval can take days to weeks
  • Larger amounts — banks can offer higher loan amounts for major expenses

When Traditional Loans Make Sense

Sometimes peer lending isn't the right fit. Here's when to consider other options.

Student Loans

For tuition, housing, and education costs that exceed what friends or family can lend. Federal student loans offer income-driven repayment plans and potential forgiveness.

Mortgage / Home Purchase

The amounts involved in buying a home are typically too large for peer lending. Mortgage lenders offer 15-30 year terms with rates often lower than personal loans.

Debt Consolidation

If you have high-interest credit card debt across multiple cards, a consolidation loan can simplify payments and reduce your overall interest rate.

Emergency Expenses

Medical bills or urgent repairs when you don't have anyone to borrow from. Some lenders offer same-day funding for emergencies.

Building Credit History

If you're establishing credit for the first time, a small personal loan with on-time payments can help build your credit score for the future.

Business Start-up

Small business loans and SBA loans are purpose-built for entrepreneurs, with terms designed for business cash flow cycles.

Understanding Your Options

A quick guide to the most common types of traditional loans.

Offered by the U.S. Department of Education. Fixed interest rates, income-driven repayment plans, deferment options, and potential loan forgiveness for public service. Start with FAFSA (fafsa.gov) to see what you qualify for. Subsidised loans don't accrue interest while you're in school.

From banks and credit unions when federal loans aren't enough. Interest rates vary by credit score (typically 4-14% APR). Often require a co-signer for students. Less flexible repayment options than federal loans. Compare offers from multiple lenders.

Unsecured loans from banks, credit unions, or online lenders. Amounts from $1,000 to $50,000+, with terms of 2-7 years. APR ranges from 6-36% depending on credit. Good for debt consolidation, large purchases, or emergency expenses. No collateral required.

Small loans ($300-$1,000) specifically designed to build credit history. The money is held in an account while you make payments. Once fully paid, the funds are released to you. Offered by many credit unions and online lenders. Great for first-time borrowers.

Borrow against the equity in your home. Lower interest rates than personal loans (typically 7-10% APR) because the loan is secured by your property. HELOCs work like a credit card with a draw period. Risk: if you can't repay, the lender can foreclose on your home.

Government-backed loans through the Small Business Administration. Lower rates and longer terms than conventional business loans. SBA 7(a) loans up to $5M, microloans up to $50K. Require a business plan and financial projections. Processing takes 2-3 months.

Before You Apply

Things to check before committing to a traditional loan.

1
Try Vony first

Ask a trusted friend or family member. You'd be surprised how often someone is willing to help, especially with clear terms and a digital agreement.

2
Check your credit score

Know where you stand before applying. Free tools like Credit Karma or annualcreditreport.com can give you your score and report.

3
Compare multiple offers

Never accept the first offer. Compare APR, fees, terms, and total cost of the loan across at least 3 lenders.

4
Read the fine print

Look for origination fees, prepayment penalties, late fees, and variable rate clauses. These can significantly increase the total cost.

5
Only borrow what you need

It's tempting to borrow more when approved for a higher amount. Stick to what you actually need to minimise interest paid.

6
Have a repayment plan

Before borrowing, calculate the monthly payment and make sure it fits your budget. Use the 50/30/20 rule as a guide.
