Loan Documents

Every Vony loan comes with real documentation you can download, share, and keep forever.

DOCUMENT 01

The Promissory Note

A promissory note is a legally recognized written promise from one person to pay another. It's the foundation of every Vony loan — a clear, signed agreement that outlines who owes what, by when, and under what terms.

Vony auto-generates your promissory note the moment both parties agree to the loan. It includes the principal amount, interest rate (if any), repayment schedule, start and end dates, and signatures from both lender and borrower. No legal jargon, no confusion — just a plain-language document that protects both sides.

Legally recognized in all 50 states
Digital signatures from both parties
Download as PDF anytime
DOCUMENT 02

Amortisation Schedule

An amortisation schedule is a complete table showing every payment across the life of a loan. Each row breaks down exactly how much of that payment goes toward the principal and how much covers interest — so both parties always know where the money goes.

Vony generates this schedule automatically based on the terms you set. It updates in real time as payments are made, so you always have an accurate picture of what's been paid, what's outstanding, and when the final payment is due. You can download it as a PDF or CSV at any time.

Everything You Need to Know

Download Anytime

Both parties can download the promissory note and amortisation schedule as a PDF from the Vony dashboard at any point during or after the loan.

Secure & Private

Documents are encrypted and stored securely. Only the lender and borrower can access them. We never share your financial information with third parties.

Always Up to Date

When a payment is made, the amortisation schedule updates automatically. You always see the most current state of the loan without manual tracking.

Plain Language

No legal jargon. Every document is written in clear, simple language that both parties can understand — because transparency is the point.

Shared Access

Both lender and borrower see the exact same documents with the exact same numbers. No asymmetry, no hidden terms — equal visibility for both sides.

Interest Handling

If you add interest, the amortisation schedule automatically calculates the principal and interest portions of each payment so nothing is ambiguous.
