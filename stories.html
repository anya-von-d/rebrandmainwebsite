Stories

Real people, real loans, real friendships preserved.

M
Marcus & Deja Atlanta, GA

When my sister Deja lost her job last spring, I didn't think twice about lending her $2,000 to cover rent while she got back on her feet. But a month in, things got weird. She'd avoid my calls, I'd wonder if she forgot, and neither of us wanted to bring it up at Sunday dinner. Money was turning us into strangers.

A coworker told me about Vony. We set up a simple repayment plan together — $250 a month, no interest, automatic reminders. Deja said it actually made her feel better because she had a clear path instead of this invisible debt hanging over her. She paid it off in eight months.

The best part wasn't getting the money back. It was getting my sister back. We don't tiptoe around each other anymore. Vony gave us a way to be honest without being awkward, and that's worth more than any dollar amount.

R
Rachel Kim San Francisco, CA

My best friend Noor and I have been close since college, so when she asked to borrow $800 for a car repair, I said yes immediately. The problem was I'd been burned before — I once lent a different friend $500 and never saw a cent of it. It didn't ruin the friendship, it just... ended it quietly. I didn't want that with Noor.

I found Vony and suggested we use it. I was nervous she'd take it the wrong way, like I didn't trust her. But she actually loved it. She said, "This is so much better than me trying to remember what I owe you." We set up biweekly payments of $100, and the app tracked everything. She even paid it off two weeks early.

Now we recommend Vony to everyone. Noor's brother used it to lend money to his roommate for a security deposit, and it went perfectly. It's funny — something designed for money actually ended up strengthening every relationship it touched.

J
James Okafor Chicago, IL

I coach a youth basketball team on the south side, and one of my players' families was about to lose their apartment. A few of us parents got together and pooled $3,500 to help them catch up on rent. The intent was beautiful. The logistics were a nightmare. Who paid what? Who was getting paid back first? Nobody wanted to be the person chasing a family in crisis for money.

One of the dads set up Vony and created separate loans from each contributor. The family could see exactly what they owed to whom, and payments were split automatically based on what each person had put in. No one had to have a single uncomfortable conversation. The dashboard showed everything — who'd been paid back, how much was left, when the next payment was due.

They paid everyone back within a year. At the end-of-season banquet, the mom stood up and thanked us — not just for the money, but for doing it in a way that let them keep their dignity. That hit different. Vony didn't just solve a money problem. It let a whole community show up for each other the right way.
